Notes on Best Configuration

The configuration relies on three ambitious pillars: Tokenization, Parameter Efficiency, and Modern Mechanisms. First, I replaced the byte-level tokenizer with a custom Byte-Pair Encoding (BPE) tokenizer (Vocab=1024), which compresses Hindi UTF-8 byte triplets into distinct tokens, dramatically extending the model's effective context length without increasing computation limits. Second, I employed Weight Tying between the embedding layer and the LM head; this reclaimed ~196K parameters, allowing me to scale the network to 4 layers and a hidden dimension of 192 while strictly respecting the 2M parameter cap. Finally, I replaced the vanilla Transformer blocks with modern Llama-style primitives: Rotary Positional Embeddings (RoPE), RMSNorm, and SwiGLU activations, which converge much faster. This architecture, paired with a Cosine Annealing Learning Rate scheduler and Gradient Accumulation to simulate a batch size of 64, forces rapid, stable convergence within the tight 2,000 step budget.
